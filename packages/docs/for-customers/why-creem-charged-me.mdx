## You have a charge from "CREEM.IO\* STORE"

This charge is from a digital product or subscription you purchased — Creem is the payment processor that handled the transaction on the seller's behalf.

Creem is a **Merchant of Record**: we process payments for independent software developers and digital product creators around the world. When you buy from one of their products, the charge on your bank or card statement shows as "CREEM.IO\* STORE" instead of the seller's name, because we are the legal entity processing the payment.

**This is normal and expected.** You were not charged by mistake.

---

## Identify the product you purchased

If you don't immediately recognize the charge, here's how to find it:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check your email">
    Search your inbox for an email from **noreply@creem.io** or **receipt@creem.io**. Your purchase confirmation includes the product name, merchant name, and amount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Look up your order">
    Go to [creem.io/my-orders/login](https://creem.io/my-orders/login) and enter the email address you used when purchasing. You'll see a list of all your Creem purchases.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Match the charge amount and date">
    In your order list, find the transaction that matches the amount and date of the charge on your statement. The merchant name and product name will be shown next to each order.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If you can't find your order with the email you tried, use a different email address — you may have purchased using a different one.

---

## I still don't recognize this charge

If you've followed the steps above and still can't identify the purchase, [contact us](https://www.creem.io/contact) and include:

- The **exact amount** of the charge
- The **date** the charge appeared on your statement
- The **last 4 digits** of the card or bank account charged

We'll look up the transaction and tell you exactly which product and merchant it came from.

---

## I want to cancel a subscription

If the charge is a recurring subscription you want to stop:

1. Go to [creem.io/my-orders/login](https://creem.io/my-orders/login) and log in with the email you used to subscribe
2. Find the active subscription and click **Cancel**

Alternatively, most merchants provide a cancellation option directly inside their product — look for a billing or account settings page.

See the full guide: [How to cancel a subscription](/for-customers/how-to-cancel-subscription)

---

## I want a refund

Refund policies are set by each merchant individually. To request a refund:

1. Contact the merchant directly using the support email on their product website or on your receipt
2. If you can't reach the merchant or they don't respond within 3 business days, [contact Creem support](https://www.creem.io/contact) — we can step in and issue a refund on the merchant's behalf

---

## I think this charge is fraudulent

If you believe someone made a purchase using your payment details without your authorization:

1. [Contact us immediately](https://www.creem.io/contact) with the charge amount, date, and last 4 digits of the card
2. Contact your bank to report the unauthorized charge

Do not file a chargeback before contacting us — in most cases we can resolve it faster directly, and it avoids the chargeback process for all parties.

---

## Why does it say "Creem" and not the seller's name?

Creem processes payments on behalf of thousands of independent developers and digital creators. As the Merchant of Record, Creem is the legal seller — which is why our name appears on your statement, not the individual developer's name.
